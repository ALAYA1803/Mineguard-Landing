In modern open-pit mining operations, high-tonnage autonomous vehicles operate on defined routes with speeds and loads that make a conventional emergency stop impossible. When a passenger-operated light vehicle invades these routes, the risk of collision is immediate and potentially fatal.

Our proposal is primarily aimed at open-pit mining companies that operate autonomous fleets, as well as drivers of light vehicles who interact with these routes. For companies, Vertex represents a solution that reduces accidents, prevents economic losses due to downtime, and strengthens compliance with safety standards. For drivers, it provides an intuitive tool that improves their awareness of their surroundings without causing distractions.

Mission

Provide IoT-based technological solutions that enable the prevention of accidents in mining environments through real-time monitoring and early warning systems, protecting the lives of workers and optimizing operational safety.

Frequently Asked Questions
How does MineGuard integrate with existing autonomous fleets? +

MineGuard uses industry-standard communication protocols (V2X) to connect with Fleet Management Systems (FMS). It is installed non-intrusively and acts as an additional safety layer that visualizes real-time data.

What is the precision level of the positioning system? +

We utilize high-precision GNSS technology with RTK corrections, allowing for centimeter-level accuracy. This is crucial for safe interaction between light vehicles and mining giants.

Does it work in areas without conventional internet coverage? +

Yes. MineGuard is designed to operate on private LTE/5G mining networks and can work in local mode (Mesh Network) to ensure that critical proximity alerts are issued even without a central server connection.

What is the implementation timeframe for the system? +

A standard implementation for a fleet of 20 light vehicles takes approximately 2 weeks, including digital environment calibration and personnel training.
